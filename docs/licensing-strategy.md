# Licensing Strategy

## Current state

Public showcase application repositories (including RetireCheck / Retirement-Calculator and SleepCheck) are **educational and architectural case studies**, typically under the **MIT License**.

This roadmap repository is also MIT-licensed documentation.

## What MIT allows

Others may copy, modify, redistribute, and use the software — including commercial use by third parties — provided license notice is preserved. That is intentional for an educational showcase.

## Irrevocability principle

Code already released under MIT **cannot be revoked** from people who obtained it under that license. You may relicense **future** versions; prior MIT snapshots remain MIT.

## Public stance

1. Keep current showcase repos as MIT educational case studies  
2. Do **not** impulsively change licenses on live showcase repos  
3. Record which code is MIT and keep copyright ownership clear  
4. Future proprietary work *may* exist separately and may not be covered by MIT  
5. Private experiments stay private — do not invent an open-core commercialization plan in this public repo  
6. Consult an attorney before dual licensing or restrictive source-available terms  

See [scope.md](./scope.md) for what this public repository will not document.

## Suggested public wording (review legally before final adoption)

> The current repository is released under the MIT License as an educational and architectural case study. Future hosted services, proprietary integrations, and other private work may be developed separately and may not be covered by the MIT License.
